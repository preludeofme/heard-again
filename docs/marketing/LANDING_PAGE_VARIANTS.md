---

**H1 Headline**
> Their voice should never be a memory. It should be *heard again.*

**Sub-headline**
> We help families capture, preserve, and relive the voices of the people they love most.

**3 Key Bullet Points**
- Record stories in their own voice
- Pass down more than photos
- Keep their presence alive forever

**Primary CTA Button Text**
> Start Preserving Voices

**Secondary CTA Button Text**
> See How It Works

**Hero Image Concept**
> A warm, intentionally blurry film-photo montage of three generations gathered around a kitchen table — an elderly grandmother laughing mid-sentence, a parent holding a phone recording, a child leaning in to listen. Soft golden-hour light spills across the scene. The image feels candid, not staged — a moment of joy being actively captured, not a stock photo of people staring at devices.

## Recommendation

| Question | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Which variant should be the control?** | **Variant A — Emotional Preservation.** It plays directly off the brand name ("heard again"), aligns with the current site's warm tone, and targets the core emotional driver that brings genealogy enthusiasts to the site in the first place. It's the safest, most brand-coherent baseline. |
| **Which should be tested first against the control?** | **Variant C — Practical / Pain-Point.** The current hero (and Variant A) both lean heavily on emotion. Variant C introduces a completely different hook — urgency and loss — which is more likely to surface a *significant* difference in conversion. If it wins, it reveals a large unrecognised user motivation. If it loses, you learn emotion is the real lever. Variant B (privacy) is a stronger secondary test for a later round, once you've validated the primary emotional or pain-point message. |

**Suggested test order:**
1. **Round 1:** A (control) vs. C (pain-point) — biggest delta hypothesis
2. **Round 2:** Winner of Round 1 vs. B (privacy/trust) — isolate whether trust is a primary or secondary driver
3. **Round 3 (optional):** All three variants simultaneously with equal traffic split if the first two rounds produce a tie or inconclusive result
